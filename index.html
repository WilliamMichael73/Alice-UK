Alice was born on August 12, 1941, in Merksem – Antwerp, into a family where sofas and furniture were shaped by hand, surrounded by the smell of wood and fabric. The daughter of artisans, she grew up among textiles, tools, and the quiet demand for work done properly. Her brother, Peter, continued the family tradition. She chose another path.

Very early on, she understood that the world would not be limited to the workshop. She had the energy of builders and the spirit of pioneers. She turned toward computing, servers, and networks — at a time when this territory was still largely male. She asked permission from no one. She learned, she mastered, she led. A strong woman: precise, fast, with that punch that silences doubt. She spoke several languages the way one opens windows. She loved to understand, to move, to connect — machines, systems, people.

In retirement, faithful to her temperament, she did not choose stillness. She decided to build a house in France, a guesthouse. A place in her image: warm, structured, demanding, and alive. She built this project with determination, and I was its manager, its accomplice, the partner in that adventure. It was not only a house. It was a discreet kingdom, a place where guests were welcomed with elegance and generosity. Alice had two children. She was a mother. Period.

But above all, she was intensely alive.

She loved wine — the good kind, the one with character. She loved good food, the kind that gathers people around a table and stretches the evening late into the night. She loved to travel, to feel cities, to listen to languages, to taste cultures. She loved sex too, without hypocrisy, with the same energy she put into everything: present, unapologetic, joyful. She was one of those people who embrace life with a full mouth.

She died on August 12, 2022, in Brasschaat, near Antwerp. On the exact day of her 81st birthday. A date almost mythological, as if her life formed a perfect circle.

Alice was not a bland woman. She was structured like a well-configured server: solid, reliable, powerful. And at the same time sensual, laughing, vibrant. Some people pass through existence. She inhabited it. And that kind of presence does not disappear. It simply changes form.

I met you in 1994. It was not a Hollywood lightning strike. It was better than that. It was a solid meeting. A meeting that stands upright. At the time, I lived in Brussels and you in Antwerp. Two cities, two rhythms, two parallel lives. You asked me to come live with you. It was not a plea; it was a clear proposal, almost strategic, the way you knew how to formulate things. And I said yes. We were friends first. Ten years of friendship. Ten years of a rare, dense, intelligent complicity. We talked about everything. We laughed a lot. We respected each other deeply. It was an adult friendship — free, vibrant. A friendship that already had the foundations of a couple, without yet carrying the name.

Then one day, naturally, almost logically, we became a couple. A solid couple. Not perfect in the naïve sense, but perfectly aligned. We understood each other. We were in sync. You with your captain’s energy, me with my ability to organize, to structure, to support. I was still working in Brussels. It wasn’t simple. Two cars. Hours on the road. Back-and-forth journeys that wear down nerves and fuel alike. But in the evening, I came back to you. And that made the kilometers bearable.

When you retired, life changed speed. You were never someone who could remain still. You decided to build that house in France, our guesthouse. A new adventure. A new playground. I was younger. I gave notice to my employer. He wasn’t happy. Too bad. Life does not negotiate with the frustrations of others. I took the reins with you.

Painting. Layout. Creating furniture. Garden. Choosing colors. The smell of wood. Soil under the fingernails. And that website I created to give a showcase to our kingdom. We were a team. A real one. Each with our strength. Each with our field. It was exciting. Physically exhausting, yes. But mentally exhilarating. We were building something concrete, beautiful, alive. We welcomed people, shared meals, opened bottles, had long late conversations. There was work, there was desire, there was laughter. What is rare is not only loving someone. What is rare is building with someone.

And with you, Alice, everything was construction. Even love.

Today I dedicate this website to her memory. Some will say it is only a website. A few pages, text, photos. Something digital, therefore intangible. Almost futile. They are wrong.

A website is a trace. An architecture of memory. A way of saying: she existed. She loved. She worked. She laughed. She built. She crossed this world with intensity. This site is not a monument of stone. It is better than that. It is alive. Accessible. Shareable. It is a presence that circulates. Just as she loved to circulate between languages and countries.

I place a beautiful text there. I add a few photographs. This very beautiful woman.

Not only beautiful in the sense of features. Beautiful in strength. Beautiful in confidence. Beautiful in that rare blend of technical intelligence and unapologetic sensuality. There is something deeply human in wanting to leave a trace. From prehistoric caves to modern servers, we carve names, faces, stories. We refuse absolute silence.

It is not futile. It is almost biological.
