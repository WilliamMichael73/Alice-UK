Alice was born on August 12, 1941, in Merksem – Antwerp, into a family where sofas and furniture were crafted by hand, surrounded by the smell of wood and fabrics. The daughter of artisans, she grew up among textiles, tools, and that silent demand for work done properly. Her brother, Peter, continued the family tradition. She chose another path. Very early on, she understood that the world would not be limited to the workshop. She had the energy of builders and the spirit of pioneers. She turned toward computing, servers, and networks — at a time when that territory was still largely male-dominated. She never asked anyone for permission. She learned, mastered, and led. A strong woman: precise, fast, with that punch that silences doubt. She spoke several languages the way others open windows. She loved understanding, moving, connecting — machines, systems, people. In retirement, true to her temperament, she did not choose stillness. She decided to build a house in France, a guesthouse. A place in her image: warm, structured, demanding, and alive. She built that project with determination, and I was its manager, accomplice, and partner in the adventure. It was not just a house. It was a discreet kingdom, a place where guests were welcomed with elegance and generosity. Alice had two children. She was a mother. Period. But above all, she was intensely alive. She loved wine the good kind, the kind with character. She loved fine food, the kind that gathers people around a table and stretches evenings late into the night. She loved traveling, feeling the pulse of cities, listening to languages, tasting cultures. She also loved sex, without hypocrisy, with the same energy she put into everything: present, confident, joyful. She was one of those people who bite deeply into life. She died on August 12, 2022, in Brasschaat, near Antwerp. On the exact day of her 81st birthday. An almost mythological date, as if her life had formed a perfect circle. Alice was not a bland woman. She was structured like a well-configured server: solid, reliable, powerful. And at the same time sensual, laughing, vibrant. Some people merely pass through existence. She inhabited it. And that kind of presence does not disappear. It simply changes form. I met you in 1994. It was not a Hollywood-style love at first sight. It was better than that. It was a solid meeting. A meeting that stood firmly on its own. At the time, I lived in Brussels and you in Antwerp. Two cities, two rhythms, two parallel lives. You asked me to come live with you. It was not a plea; it was a clear proposal, almost strategic, the way you knew so well how to formulate things. And I said yes. We were friends first. Ten years of friendship. Ten years of a rare, deep, intelligent connection. We talked about everything. We laughed a lot. We respected each other deeply. It was an adult friendship — free, vibrant. A friendship that already carried the foundations of a couple, without yet bearing the name. Then one day, naturally, almost logically, we became a couple. A solid couple. Not perfect in the naïve sense of the word, but perfectly aligned. We understood each other. We were synchronized. You with your captain’s energy, me with my ability to organize, structure, and support. I was still working in Brussels. It was not easy. Two cars. Endless hours on the road. Back-and-forth journeys that wear down both nerves and fuel. But every evening, I came back to you. And that made the kilometers bearable. When you retired, life changed rhythm. You had never been someone capable of standing still. You decided to build that house in France, our guesthouse. A new adventure. A new playground. I was younger. I resigned from my employer. He was not pleased. Too bad. Life does not negotiate with other people’s frustrations. I took command alongside you. Painting. Interior design. Furniture creation. Gardening. Choosing colors. The smell of wood. Earth under our fingernails. And that website I created to give our kingdom a showcase. We were a team. A real one. Each with our own strength. Each with our own domain. It was exciting. Physically exhausting, yes. But mentally exhilarating. We were building something concrete, beautiful, alive. We welcomed people, shared meals, opened bottles, had long late-night conversations. There was work, desire, laughter. What is rare is not only loving someone. What is rare is building with someone. And with you, Alice, everything was construction. Even love. Today, I dedicate this website to her memory. Some will say it is only a website. A few pages, text, photographs. Something digital, therefore intangible. Almost futile. They are wrong. A website is a trace. An architecture of memory. A way of saying: she existed. She loved. She worked. She laughed. She built. She crossed through this world intensely. This website is not a monument of stone. It is better than that. It is alive. Accessible. Shareable. It is a presence that circulates. Exactly as she loved to circulate between languages and countries. I place a beautiful text there. I add a few photographs. This very beautiful woman. Beautiful not only in her features. Beautiful in her strength. Beautiful in her confidence. Beautiful in that rare combination of technical intelligence and unapologetic sensuality. There is something profoundly human in the desire to leave a trace. From prehistoric caves to modern servers, we carve names, faces, stories. We refuse absolute silence. It is not futile. It is almost biological.
